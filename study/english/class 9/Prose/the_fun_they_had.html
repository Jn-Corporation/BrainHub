Chapter Summary

Conceptual Q&A Matrix

Q1. What kind of schools did Margie and Tommy have in the story?
Margie and Tommy had highly personalized mechanical schools right inside their homes. They didn't have regular human teachers; instead, they were taught by complex mechanical robotic monitors customized to their individual learning metrics.
Q2. Why did Margie hate her mechanical school setups?
Margie hated her school because it was dull, automated, and lonely. The geography sector of her mechanical teacher was running too fast, repeatedly giving her bad test marks, and she despised the slot where she had to punch in her homework every day.
Q3. What was strange or unique about the "real book" Tommy found?
The most unique thing about the real paper book was that its words stood perfectly still instead of moving like the text on a telebook screen. Also, the pages turned yellow and crinkly, and reading the same words again yielded the exact same result.

About the Writer

Isaac Asimov

Isaac Asimov (1920–1992) was an exceptionally prolific American writer and professor of biochemistry, famously recognized as one of the founding pillars of hard science fiction literature. His stories elegantly speculate on technological paradigms, robotic controls, and future human adaptations.
