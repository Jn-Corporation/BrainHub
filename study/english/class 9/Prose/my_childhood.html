Conceptual Q&A Matrix

📘 Short Answer Questions (20–30 Words)

Q1. Where was A.P.J. Abdul Kalam born?
A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born in Rameswaram, a small island town in Tamil Nadu. It was famous for its religious and cultural harmony.
Q2. What was the profession of Kalam's father?
Kalam's father, Jainulabdeen, owned boats that carried pilgrims between Rameswaram and Dhanushkodi. He was respected for his wisdom and simplicity.
Q3. Who was Ahmed Jalaluddin?
Ahmed Jalaluddin was Kalam's close friend and relative who inspired him by discussing science, literature, and the outside world.
Q4. How did Kalam earn his first wages?
Kalam earned his first wages by helping his cousin Samsuddin distribute newspapers during the Second World War.
Q5. Who was Ramanadha Sastry?
Ramanadha Sastry was Kalam's close friend and the son of the chief priest of the Rameswaram Temple.
Q6. Why was Kalam asked to sit on the back bench?
A new teacher considered it inappropriate for a Muslim boy to sit beside a Hindu priest's son and shifted Kalam to the back bench.
Q7. Who was Sivasubramania Iyer?
Sivasubramania Iyer was Kalam's science teacher who encouraged scientific thinking and fought against social discrimination.
Q8. How did Lakshmana Sastry react to the teacher's behavior?
Lakshmana Sastry strongly criticized the teacher and asked him to apologize for spreading social discrimination among children.
Q9. What values did Kalam learn from his father?
Kalam learned honesty, simplicity, self-discipline, faith in God, and respect for all human beings from his father.
Q10. What is the central message of "My Childhood"?
The chapter teaches the importance of religious harmony, tolerance, equality, hard work, and good values in life.

📝 Long Answer Questions (60–70 Words)

Q11. Describe Kalam's family background.
A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born into a middle-class Tamil Muslim family in Rameswaram. His father, Jainulabdeen, was a boat owner known for his wisdom and simplicity. His mother, Ashiamma, was kind and caring. Though the family was not wealthy, they lived a comfortable life based on strong moral values, honesty, hard work, and religious faith.
Q12. How did Ahmed Jalaluddin influence Kalam's life?
Ahmed Jalaluddin played a significant role in shaping Kalam's thinking. He encouraged him to dream big and look beyond the small world of Rameswaram. Their discussions about science, literature, and global events broadened Kalam's knowledge and curiosity. Jalaluddin inspired him to believe in education and progress, which later helped him achieve great success.
Q13. Explain the incident of discrimination in Kalam's classroom.
One day, a new teacher noticed Kalam, a Muslim boy, sitting beside Ramanadha Sastry, a Hindu priest's son. The teacher disapproved and asked Kalam to move to the back bench. This hurt both boys deeply. When Lakshmana Sastry learned about the incident, he scolded the teacher and forced him to apologize, teaching a valuable lesson against discrimination.
Q14. How did Sivasubramania Iyer help Kalam overcome social barriers?
Sivasubramania Iyer was a progressive science teacher who believed in equality. He invited Kalam to his home for a meal despite social prejudices. When his wife refused to serve Kalam because of his religion, Iyer personally served him. Later, his wife changed her attitude and served Kalam herself. This experience taught Kalam the importance of social reform and courage.
Q15. What lessons did Kalam learn from his childhood?
Kalam learned valuable lessons from his childhood experiences. He understood the importance of hard work, discipline, honesty, and faith. He also learned to respect all religions and cultures. The support of his family, teachers, and friends helped him develop confidence and determination. These lessons became the foundation of his success as a scientist and national leader.
Q16. Describe the communal harmony in Rameswaram.
Rameswaram was a place where people of different religions lived together peacefully. Kalam's father, the temple priest Pakshi Lakshmana Sastry, and a church priest shared mutual respect and friendship. Children from different religious backgrounds studied and played together. This atmosphere of unity and cooperation deeply influenced Kalam and taught him the values of tolerance and harmony.
Q17. Why is "My Childhood" an inspiring chapter?
The chapter is inspiring because it shows how a simple boy from a modest family achieved greatness through hard work and determination. Kalam overcame social barriers and financial limitations with the help of supportive family members, friends, and teachers. His story motivates students to dream big, work sincerely, and remain humble regardless of success.
Q18. Discuss the role of teachers in Kalam's life.
Teachers played an important role in Kalam's development. While one teacher showed prejudice, others like Sivasubramania Iyer guided and inspired him. Iyer encouraged scientific thinking and challenged social discrimination. These experiences taught Kalam important lessons about equality, education, and perseverance. His teachers helped shape his character and future achievements.
Q19. How did Kalam's parents influence his character?
Kalam's parents greatly influenced his personality. His father taught him honesty, simplicity, and faith, while his mother showed kindness and compassion. Their values created a strong moral foundation for him. Through their example, Kalam learned to respect others, remain humble, and face life's challenges with courage and determination.
Q20. What qualities helped Kalam become successful in life?
Kalam's success was built on hard work, discipline, curiosity, determination, and positive thinking. He remained dedicated to learning and never allowed difficulties to discourage him. The guidance of his family, friends, and teachers strengthened his character. His commitment to knowledge and service eventually helped him become a renowned scientist and President of India.

⭐ Very Long Answer Questions

Character Sketch Questions

Q21. Character Sketch of A.P.J. Abdul Kalam.
A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was a hardworking, humble, curious, and determined child. He respected all religions and valued education greatly. Despite belonging to a modest family, he dreamed big and worked sincerely to achieve his goals. Influenced by his parents, teachers, and friends, he developed qualities such as honesty, discipline, perseverance, and compassion. These traits later helped him become one of India's most respected scientists and leaders.
Q22. Character Sketch of Jainulabdeen (Kalam's Father).
Jainulabdeen was a wise, simple, honest, and deeply spiritual person. Though not formally educated, he possessed great practical wisdom. He believed in helping others and living a disciplined life. He taught Kalam important values such as humility, faith, honesty, and self-respect. His calm nature and strong moral principles greatly influenced Kalam's personality and future success.

Theme-Based Questions

Q23. Discuss the theme of religious harmony in "My Childhood".
Religious harmony is one of the central themes of the chapter. People from different religions in Rameswaram respected and supported one another. Kalam's father, the temple priest, and the church priest maintained close friendships. Even when discrimination appeared, wise elders corrected it. Through these experiences, Kalam learned that humanity is more important than religious differences and that unity strengthens society.
Q24. How do family, friends, and teachers contribute to Kalam's success?
Kalam's success was the result of support from many people. His parents gave him strong moral values. Jalaluddin inspired him to dream beyond his surroundings. Friends taught him cooperation and understanding, while teachers encouraged scientific thinking and equality. Together, these influences shaped his character, broadened his knowledge, and motivated him to achieve excellence in life.
Q25. What life lessons can students learn from "My Childhood"?
Students can learn many valuable lessons from the chapter. They should respect all religions, work hard, remain disciplined, and never stop learning. The chapter teaches that success comes from determination, good values, and positive guidance. It also emphasizes the importance of equality, tolerance, and strong character. Kalam's life proves that even a child from a simple background can achieve extraordinary success.

About the Writer

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (1931–2015) was one of India's most respected scientists, authors, and leaders. His full name was Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam. He was born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, in a humble Muslim family. Known as the "Missile Man of India", he played a key role in India's missile and space programs and made significant contributions to the country's defense technology. Dr. Kalam served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007 and was widely loved as the "People's President". He inspired millions of students through his speeches, books, and vision for a developed India. Some of his famous works include Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, India 2020, and My Journey. The chapter "My Childhood" is an extract from his autobiography Wings of Fire and reflects his early life, strong values, religious harmony, and the experiences that shaped his remarkable personality.
