Conceptual Q&A Matrix

Q1. Who was the narrator of "The Snake and the Mirror"?
The narrator was a young homeopathic doctor who lived alone in a small rented room. He later narrated his frightening yet humorous experience with a snake.
Q2. Where did the doctor live?
The doctor lived in a small rented room that was poorly furnished and surrounded by trees. It was an old house with many rats.
Q3. What was the doctor's financial condition?
The doctor was not financially well-off. He had only sixty rupees in his suitcase and a few coins in his pocket.
Q4. What was hanging on the wall of the doctor's room?
A large mirror was hanging on the wall above a small table. The doctor often looked into it and admired himself.
Q5. What did the doctor decide while looking into the mirror?
The doctor decided to shave daily, grow a thin moustache, and look more handsome. He also planned to marry a rich and fat woman doctor.
Q6. Why did the doctor want to marry a fat woman doctor?
He thought a fat woman would not be able to run after him and catch him if he made a mistake after marriage.
Q7. What suddenly fell on the doctor's shoulder?
A snake suddenly fell from the roof onto the doctor's shoulder while he was sitting near the table.
Q8. Where did the snake settle itself?
The snake coiled around the doctor's left arm and neck and rested its head near his shoulder.
Q9. How did the doctor feel when he saw the snake?
The doctor was terrified. His body became stiff, and he could not move because he feared that any movement might provoke the snake.
Q10. Why could the doctor not move?
The doctor could not move because he was frozen with fear. He knew that even a slight movement could cause the snake to bite him.
Q11. What did the doctor think about himself during the incident?
The doctor realized how powerless and insignificant he was before nature. His pride and self-admiration disappeared completely.
Q12. What did the doctor pray for?
The doctor silently prayed to God for safety and promised to become a better person if he survived the dangerous situation.
Q13. What attracted the snake toward the mirror?
The snake was attracted by its own reflection in the mirror. It seemed fascinated by the image it saw.
Q14. What did the snake do after seeing its reflection?
The snake slowly moved away from the doctor's body and slithered toward the mirror to look at its reflection.
Q15. How did the doctor escape from the room?
As soon as the snake moved toward the mirror, the doctor quietly jumped up and ran out of the room as fast as he could.
Q16. What happened when the doctor returned to his room?
When the doctor returned later with friends, he discovered that a thief had stolen almost all of his belongings.
Q17. What was the only thing left in the room?
The thief had taken everything except a dirty vest, which was considered worthless.
Q18. Why is the story considered humorous?
The story is humorous because of the doctor's vanity, his funny thoughts about marriage, and the ironic situations that occur throughout the incident.
Q19. What lesson does the story teach?
The story teaches that pride can disappear in moments of danger and that life is unpredictable. It also highlights the power of nature.
Q20. Who wrote "The Snake and the Mirror"?
"The Snake and the Mirror" was written by Vaikom Muhammad Basheer, a famous Malayalam writer known for his humorous and realistic stories.
Q21. Character Sketch of the Doctor.
Introduction:
The doctor is the central character and narrator of the story "The Snake and the Mirror." He is a young, unmarried homeopathic doctor who lives alone in a small rented room. Through his actions and thoughts, readers get a clear picture of his personality.

Young and Ambitious:
The doctor was at the beginning of his career and dreamed of a successful future. Although he was poor at that time, he hoped to become wealthy and respected one day.

Vain and Self-Admiring:
He was very conscious of his appearance. He spent time looking into the mirror, admiring himself, and planning to improve his looks by shaving regularly and growing a thin moustache.

Humorous and Imaginative:
The doctor had a good sense of humor. His thoughts about marrying a fat woman doctor who would not be able to chase him reveal his playful and imaginative nature.

Fearful but Wise:
When the snake coiled around his neck, he was terrified. However, he remained completely still and avoided making any sudden movements, which ultimately saved his life.

Conclusion:
The doctor is an interesting blend of vanity, humor, intelligence, and fear. His experience with the snake teaches him humility and helps him understand the unpredictability of life.
Q22. Describe the Snake Incident in Detail.
Introduction:
The snake incident forms the most exciting and dramatic part of the story. It completely changes the doctor's mood and outlook within a few moments.

Before the Incident:
One hot summer night, the doctor was sitting in his room. He was admiring himself in the mirror and making plans about his future appearance and marriage.

The Arrival of the Snake:
Suddenly, a snake fell from the roof onto his shoulder. Within moments, it coiled itself around his arm and neck. The doctor was shocked and frozen with fear.

The Doctor's Fear:
The doctor realized that even a small movement could provoke the snake to bite him. He remained motionless and silently prayed for his safety while thinking about his mistakes and weaknesses.

The Escape:
The snake eventually became interested in its reflection in the mirror. It slowly moved away from the doctor's body and slithered toward the mirror. Seizing the opportunity, the doctor rushed out of the room and escaped.

Conclusion:
The incident was both frightening and humorous. It demonstrated the doctor's vulnerability and highlighted how quickly circumstances can change in life.
Q23. Theme, Message, and Central Idea of "The Snake and the Mirror".
Introduction:
"The Snake and the Mirror" is an entertaining story that combines humor, suspense, and valuable life lessons. Through a simple incident, the author conveys several important messages.

Human Vanity:
One of the major themes of the story is vanity. Before the snake appeared, the doctor was busy admiring his appearance and dreaming about his future. His pride disappeared the moment he faced danger.

Fear and Survival:
The story shows how fear affects human behavior. The doctor became completely still and obedient to the situation because his survival depended on remaining calm.

Power of Nature:
The snake symbolizes the power of nature. Despite being educated and intelligent, the doctor became helpless in front of a small creature.

Unpredictability of Life:
Life can change suddenly and unexpectedly. One moment the doctor was dreaming about his future, and the next moment he was facing a life-threatening situation.

Conclusion:
The story teaches us to remain humble, respect nature, and understand that life is full of unexpected events that can change our perspective instantly.
Q24. How Does the Author Create Humor and Suspense in the Story?
Introduction:
Vaikom Muhammad Basheer skillfully combines humor and suspense to make the story interesting and memorable. Readers experience laughter and tension at the same time.

Humorous Elements:
The doctor's admiration for his appearance and his funny plans about marrying a fat woman doctor create humor. His confidence and vanity make readers smile.

Suspenseful Situation:
The suspense begins when the snake suddenly falls onto the doctor's shoulder. Readers become anxious because they do not know whether the doctor will survive.

Emotional Contrast:
The contrast between the doctor's earlier confidence and his later helplessness increases both humor and suspense. His sudden transformation is highly dramatic.

Unexpected Ending:
The snake's fascination with its reflection and the theft that occurs afterward create an ironic and amusing conclusion.

Conclusion:
Through clever narration, irony, and unexpected events, the author successfully blends humor and suspense, making the story both entertaining and meaningful.
Q25. Explain the Significance of the Title "The Snake and the Mirror".
Introduction:
The title "The Snake and the Mirror" is highly appropriate because both the snake and the mirror play important roles in the story and contribute to its deeper meaning.

Importance of the Mirror:
The mirror symbolizes vanity and self-admiration. Before the snake appears, the doctor spends time admiring his appearance and dreaming about becoming more attractive.

Importance of the Snake:
The snake symbolizes danger, fear, and the unpredictable forces of nature. Its sudden appearance completely changes the doctor's thoughts and behavior.

Connection Between the Two:
Ironically, the same mirror that encouraged the doctor's vanity later saves his life. The snake becomes fascinated by its own reflection, allowing the doctor to escape.

Symbolic Meaning:
Together, the snake and the mirror represent the contrast between human pride and reality. The doctor learns that life is uncertain and that pride can disappear instantly when faced with danger.

Conclusion:
The title perfectly captures the central events and themes of the story. It highlights vanity, fear, self-realization, and the unpredictable nature of life, making it meaningful and memorable.

About the Writer

Vaikom Muhammad Basheer

Vaikom Muhammad Basheer (1908–1994) was one of the most celebrated writers in Malayalam literature and is widely regarded as a pioneer of modern Indian storytelling. Born in Vaikom, Kerala, he actively participated in India's freedom movement and spent several years traveling across the country, gaining rich life experiences that later influenced his writings. Basheer's works are admired for their simplicity, humor, realism, and deep understanding of human nature. He wrote about ordinary people, their struggles, emotions, relationships, and everyday life in a unique and engaging style. Some of his most famous works include Balyakalasakhi, Pathummayude Aadu, Mathilukal, and Ntuppuppakkoranendarnnu. The story "The Snake and the Mirror" beautifully showcases his talent for blending humor, suspense, and life lessons while exploring themes such as vanity, fear, self-realization, and the unpredictable nature of life.
