Conceptual Q&A Matrix

Q1. Who is the poet of the poem "Wind"?
The poem "Wind" is written by Subramania Bharati, a famous Tamil poet and freedom fighter. His poems often inspire courage, strength, and self-confidence.
Q2. Why does the poet ask the wind not to break the shutters of the windows?
The poet asks the wind not to break the shutters because strong winds can cause destruction and damage. He speaks to the wind as if it were a living being.
Q3. What damage does the wind cause in the poem?
The wind scatters papers, throws down books, tears pages, and destroys weak houses, doors, rafters, and trees. It creates disorder and destruction wherever things are weak.
Q4. What does the wind symbolize in the poem?
The wind symbolizes hardships, challenges, and difficulties in life. Just as strong winds test physical objects, challenges test a person's strength and determination.
Q5. Why does the wind destroy weak things?
The wind destroys weak things because they are unable to withstand its force. This teaches us that weakness attracts difficulties, while strength helps us overcome them.
Q6. How can we make friends with the wind?
We can make friends with the wind by becoming strong and prepared. The poet advises us to build strong homes, strong bodies, and strong hearts to face challenges confidently.
Q7. What happens to strong fires when the wind blows?
Strong fires become stronger when the wind blows. This shows that difficulties can help strong and determined people grow and become more powerful.
Q8. What message does the poet give through the poem?
The poet gives the message that we should become strong enough to face life's challenges. Instead of fearing difficulties, we should use them as opportunities to grow.
Q9. What is the central idea of the poem "Wind"?
The central idea of the poem is that challenges and hardships are a part of life. People who are strong, courageous, and determined can overcome difficulties and succeed.
Q10. Why is the poem "Wind" considered motivational?
The poem is considered motivational because it encourages readers to develop inner strength, confidence, and resilience. It teaches that obstacles can make us stronger if we face them with courage.

About the Writer

Subramania Bharati

Subramania Bharati (1882–1921), popularly known as Bharathiyar, was one of the greatest Tamil poets, writers, journalists, and Indian freedom fighters. He is celebrated for his patriotic poems, revolutionary ideas, and inspiring writings that encouraged people to fight against British rule. Bharati's works focus on courage, strength, equality, women's empowerment, social justice, and national unity. His poetry is known for its powerful language, emotional depth, and motivational spirit. Some of his famous works include Kuyil Pattu, Panchali Sapatham, Kannan Pattu, and numerous patriotic songs. The poem "Wind" reflects his belief in inner strength and resilience, teaching readers that challenges make us stronger and help us grow into better individuals.
