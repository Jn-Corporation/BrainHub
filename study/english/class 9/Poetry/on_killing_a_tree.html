Conceptual Q&A Matrix

Q1. Who is the poet of "On Killing a Tree"?
"On Killing a Tree" is written by Gieve Patel, a renowned Indian poet, playwright, painter, and physician. His works often focus on human life and nature.
Q2. What is the central idea of the poem "On Killing a Tree"?
The poem highlights the strength and resilience of nature. It shows that a tree cannot be destroyed easily because it has deep roots and a strong will to survive.
Q3. Why does the poet say that a simple jab of a knife cannot kill a tree?
A simple jab of a knife cannot kill a tree because it has grown over many years and possesses immense strength. Even after being cut, it can grow again from its roots.
Q4. How does a tree grow and become strong?
A tree grows slowly over many years by absorbing water, sunlight, air, and nutrients from the soil. This continuous nourishment makes it strong and capable of surviving hardships.
Q5. What happens when a tree is chopped down?
When a tree is chopped down, it does not die immediately. New shoots and small twigs begin to grow again from the remaining roots and trunk.
Q6. Which part of the tree is responsible for its survival?
The roots are responsible for the tree's survival. Hidden deep inside the earth, they continue supplying nourishment and help the tree regenerate.
Q7. What must be done to kill a tree completely?
To kill a tree completely, it must be uprooted from the earth. Its roots must be pulled out and exposed to the sun and air until they dry up and die.
Q8. Why are the roots compared to the source of the tree's life?
The roots are compared to the source of the tree's life because they absorb nutrients and water from the soil, enabling the tree to grow and survive.
Q9. What does the expression "bleeding bark" suggest?
The expression "bleeding bark" personifies the tree and suggests that it feels pain when injured, making readers sympathize with nature.
Q10. How does the poem reflect the power of nature?
The poem reflects the power of nature by showing that even after severe damage, a tree continues to fight for survival and regenerate itself.
Q11. What message does the poet give through the poem?
The poet conveys that nature is strong and resilient. He indirectly encourages people to respect trees and avoid unnecessary destruction of the environment.
Q12. How does the poet create sympathy for the tree?
The poet uses vivid imagery and personification, such as describing the bark as bleeding, to make readers feel the suffering of the tree and develop sympathy for it.
Q13. What literary device is used when the tree is described as bleeding?
The literary device used is personification. The tree is given human qualities to emphasize its living nature and the pain caused by its destruction.
Q14. Why is the poem considered an environmental poem?
The poem is considered an environmental poem because it highlights the importance of trees, their struggle for survival, and the need to protect nature from destruction.
Q15. What lesson do we learn from "On Killing a Tree"?
The poem teaches us to value and protect nature. It reminds us that trees are living beings with great strength and that environmental conservation is essential for a healthy planet.

About the Writer

Gieve Patel

Gieve Patel (1940–2023) was an Indian poet, playwright, painter, and physician. He is known for his sensitive and thought-provoking poetry that often focuses on nature, human responsibility, and environmental concerns. Patel’s writing reflects a deep awareness of life, death, and the impact of human actions on the natural world. Apart from poetry, he was also a successful painter and a practicing doctor, which gave his works a unique blend of scientific observation and artistic imagination. His notable works include Poems, Mirrored, Mirroring, and On Killing a Tree, where he powerfully highlights the resilience of nature and the cruelty of destroying it.
