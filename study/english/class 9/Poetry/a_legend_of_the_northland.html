A Legend of the Northland

A Legend of the Northland is a captivating poem that tells the story of a brave woman who defies societal norms and expectations. Through vivid imagery and powerful language, the poem explores themes of courage, resilience, and the strength of the human spirit. 🎵

Conceptual Q&A Matrix

Q1. What is the Northland described in the poem?
The Northland is a cold northern region covered with snow and ice. According to the poem, the days are very short there in winter, and children travel on reindeer sledges.
Q2. Who came to the little woman’s cottage and why?
A saint came to the little woman’s cottage. He was tired and hungry after fasting for many days and requested her to give him a small cake to eat.
Q3. What was the little woman doing when the saint arrived?
When the saint arrived, the little woman was baking cakes on a hearth. She was preparing food for herself but was unwilling to share it with the hungry saint.
Q4. Why did the woman not give the first cake to the saint?
The woman did not give the first cake because she thought it was too large to part with. Her selfish nature prevented her from helping the hungry saint.
Q5. How did the woman behave with the saint?
The woman behaved selfishly and greedily. Although the saint only asked for a small cake, she repeatedly refused to give him any proper cake and finally offered only a tiny scrap.
Q6. What punishment did the saint give to the woman?
The saint punished the woman by turning her into a woodpecker. He sent her away into the forest to search for food by pecking at trees throughout her life.
Q7. How did the woman change into a woodpecker?
The woman's clothes changed into feathers, her nose became a long beak, and she turned into a woodpecker. Only her red cap remained on her head.
Q8. What does the woodpecker do in the forest?
The woodpecker lives in the forest and pecks at tree trunks throughout the day. It searches for worms hidden inside the wood to satisfy its hunger.
Q9. Why is the poem called “A Legend of the Northland”?
The poem is called a legend because it tells a traditional folk tale passed down through generations. It explains in an imaginative way how the woodpecker came into existence.
Q10. What moral lesson does the poem teach?
The poem teaches that greed and selfishness lead to punishment, while kindness and generosity are rewarded. We should always help those who are in need.

About the Writer

Phoebe Cary

Phoebe Cary (1824–1871) was a well-known American poet and writer of the nineteenth century. She was born in Ohio, USA, and gained popularity for her simple, moral, and inspiring poems. Phoebe Cary often wrote about kindness, generosity, faith, and human values. Along with her sister, Alice Cary, she became one of the most respected literary figures of her time. Her poetry is admired for its clear language, strong moral lessons, and imaginative storytelling. The poem "A Legend of the Northland" is one of her famous works, in which she uses a traditional folk tale to teach the importance of kindness and the dangers of greed and selfishness.
