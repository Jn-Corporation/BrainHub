Conceptual Q&A Matrix

Q1. Where did the child go with his parents?
The child went to a village fair with his parents.
Q2. Who wrote “The Lost Child”?
It is written by Mulk Raj Anand.
Q3. What attracted the child at the fair?
The child was attracted to toys, sweets, and fair attractions.
Q4. What did the child want first?
The child first wanted a toy balloon.
Q5. What ride did the child want?
The child wanted to ride the merry-go-round.
Q6. What happened to the child in the fair?
The child got lost from his parents in the fair.
Q7. Who helped the lost child?
A kind man helped the lost child.
Q8. What did the child ask for repeatedly?
He kept asking for his parents.
Q9. What did the man try to give the child?
The man tried to distract him with toys and sweets.
Q10. What is the main theme of the story?
The main theme is love and importance of parents.
Q11. Describe what attracted the child in the fair.
The child was deeply attracted by the colorful and joyful atmosphere of the fair. He wanted toys, sweets, balloons, and rides like the merry-go-round. Everything looked exciting to him, and he kept stopping to look at different things, forgetting that his parents were walking ahead. His curiosity and innocence made him want everything he saw.
Q12. Why did the child get lost?
The child got lost because he was busy looking at the fair attractions and moved away from his parents. His attention kept shifting from one thing to another, and he did not notice when he was separated. In the crowded fair, he could not find his parents again, which made him scared and helpless.
Q13. How did the child react after getting lost?
After getting lost, the child became very frightened and started crying. He ran around searching for his parents and called out for them. All the joy of the fair disappeared, and he refused every attraction offered to him. His only desire was to find his parents and feel safe again.
Q14. How did the stranger try to help the child?
The stranger tried to calm the child by offering him toys, sweets, balloons, and rides that he had earlier desired. However, the child refused everything because he only wanted his parents. The man realized that the child’s emotional pain was deeper than material attractions and tried to comfort him gently.
Q15. What message does the story give?
The story teaches that parental love is the most important thing in a child’s life. Material things like toys, sweets, and rides cannot replace the comfort and security given by parents. It shows the innocence of childhood and how children realize the true value of their parents only when they are separated from them.
Q16. How is the fair described in the story?
The fair is described as a lively and colorful place full of excitement, noise, and crowd. There are many attractions like toys, sweets, balloons, and rides. It creates a joyful atmosphere for children, but at the same time, it is so crowded that it becomes easy for someone to get separated from their family.
Q17. Why did the child refuse all offers after getting lost?
After getting lost, the child refused all offers because his emotional state had changed completely. Earlier he wanted toys and sweets, but now he was scared and only wanted his parents. His fear and separation made him lose interest in all material things, showing the strong bond between child and parents.
Q18. What role does innocence play in the story?
Innocence plays a very important role in the story. The child is innocent and easily attracted to beautiful things in the fair. He does not understand danger or separation initially. However, his innocence also shows his deep emotional need for his parents, which becomes clear when he gets lost and feels helpless.
Q19. What does the stranger realize about the child?
The stranger realizes that the child is not interested in toys or sweets anymore because he is emotionally disturbed. The child only wants his parents. The stranger understands that emotional security is more important than material things and shows kindness by trying to comfort and protect the child in the crowd.
Q20. What is the central idea of the story?
The central idea of the story is the deep love and emotional bond between parents and children. It shows that no attraction in the world can replace the comfort of parents. The story highlights how children often realize the true value of their parents only when they are separated from them in difficult situations.
Q21. Character Sketch of the Lost Child
Introduction: The child in the story is a symbol of innocence and curiosity.

Nature: He is innocent, sensitive, and easily attracted to colorful things in the fair. He gets excited by toys, sweets, and rides, showing his childlike nature.

Emotional Side: When he gets separated from his parents, he becomes extremely scared and emotional. All his desires vanish, and he only wants his parents back.

Conclusion: The child represents pure innocence and shows that parental love is the most important need in life.
Q22. Character Sketch of the Parents
Introduction: The parents play a silent but powerful role in the story.

Nature: They are caring, responsible, and protective towards their child. They take him to the fair and keep him close initially.

Role in Story: Even though they are not shown much directly after the child gets lost, their importance increases. The child’s fear shows how much he depends on them.

Conclusion: They represent safety, love, and emotional security in a child’s life.
Q23. Character Sketch of the Kind Stranger
Introduction: The stranger is a minor but important character in the story.

Nature: He is kind, helpful, and understanding. He notices the lost child and tries to help him calmly.

Actions: He offers the child toys, sweets, and other attractions to distract him. However, he realizes that the child only wants his parents.

Conclusion: He represents human kindness and emotional understanding in society.
Q24. The Theme of “The Lost Child”
Introduction: The story revolves around a simple but powerful theme of love and separation.

Main Idea: It shows that children are easily attracted to material things like toys, sweets, and fairs, but their real emotional need is their parents.

Message: The story teaches that no attraction in the world can replace parental love and security. It also highlights innocence and emotional dependence.

Conclusion: The theme strongly focuses on family bonding and emotional importance of parents.
Q25. The Fair as a Symbol in the Story
Introduction: The fair in the story is not just a place, but a symbol of attraction and temptation.

Description: It is full of colorful stalls, toys, sweets, rides, and noise, representing excitement and joy for children.

Symbolism: It symbolizes worldly attractions that distract humans from real priorities. The child gets lost because of these distractions.

Conclusion: The fair represents life’s temptations, while the child’s final realization shows the importance of emotional bonds over material things.
Q26. Character Sketch of the Lost Child
Introduction: The lost child is the central character of the story and represents innocence and emotional purity.

Nature and Personality: He is a young, innocent, and curious child who gets easily attracted to the colorful attractions of the fair. His mind is full of excitement as he sees toys, sweets, balloons, and rides. He behaves like a typical child who wants to enjoy everything around him without understanding consequences.

Emotional Transformation: When he gets separated from his parents, his entire behavior changes. He forgets all attractions and becomes scared, helpless, and emotionally disturbed. His only desire is to find his parents again.

Conclusion: The child symbolizes pure innocence and shows that parental love and security are more important than any material attraction in the world.
Q27. Character Sketch of the Parents
Introduction: The parents in the story play a quiet but extremely important role in shaping the emotional depth of the narrative.

Nature and Behavior: They are caring, responsible, and protective towards their child. They take him to the fair with love and attention, ensuring he stays close to them in the beginning. Their presence represents safety and emotional security for the child.

Importance in Story: Although they are not directly shown after the child gets lost, their importance becomes stronger through the child’s fear and desperation. The child’s repeated cries for them show his deep dependence on them.

Conclusion: The parents symbolize unconditional love, protection, and emotional stability in a child’s life, highlighting the strong bond between family members.
Q28. Character Sketch of the Kind Stranger
Introduction: The stranger is a minor but meaningful character who represents kindness and humanity in the story.

Nature and Personality: He is observant, gentle, and compassionate. When he notices the lost child crying in panic, he immediately steps forward to help him without expecting anything in return. His behavior shows empathy and responsibility towards a helpless child.

Actions: He tries to comfort the child by offering him toys, sweets, balloons, and rides that earlier attracted him. However, he soon realizes that the child is emotionally distressed and only wants his parents, not material things.

Conclusion: The stranger symbolizes human kindness and emotional understanding, showing that compassion exists even among unknown people in society.
Q29. The Central Theme of “The Lost Child”
Introduction: The story “The Lost Child” revolves around a simple incident but carries a deep emotional and moral message.

Main Idea: The central theme focuses on the contrast between material attractions and emotional needs. The child is initially attracted to toys, sweets, and fair activities, but when he gets lost, all these desires disappear, and he only longs for his parents.

Message: The story beautifully conveys that parental love is the strongest emotional bond and cannot be replaced by any worldly attraction. It also highlights innocence and dependency of children.

Conclusion: The theme teaches that real happiness lies in love, safety, and family bonding rather than material enjoyment.
Q30. The Fair as a Symbol and Its Importance in the Story
Introduction: The fair in “The Lost Child” is not just a physical place but a powerful symbol used by the author to convey deeper meaning.

Description: The fair is full of colors, noise, excitement, and attractions such as toys, sweets, balloons, and rides. It represents joy and entertainment, especially for children.

Symbolism: The fair symbolizes worldly temptations and material distractions that easily attract human attention. The child’s distraction and eventual separation from his parents show how easily one can lose focus in such environments.

Conclusion: The fair ultimately represents life’s distractions, while the child’s realization shows that emotional bonds are far more important than temporary attractions.

About the Writer

Mulk Raj Anand

Mulk Raj Anand (1905–2004) was one of the pioneering Indian English writers and a major figure in modern Indian literature. He is widely known for his realistic and socially conscious writing style that highlights the struggles of common people. Anand strongly focused on themes like poverty, inequality, human emotions, and social injustice. His works often reflect deep empathy for the underprivileged and bring attention to human values and relationships. Some of his famous works include Untouchable, Coolie, and Two Leaves and a Bud. The chapter “The Lost Child” showcases his ability to express deep emotional experiences through the innocent perspective of a child, highlighting love, separation, and the importance of parents in a child’s life.
