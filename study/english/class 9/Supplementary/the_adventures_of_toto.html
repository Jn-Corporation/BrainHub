Conceptual Q&A Matrix

Q1. Who was Toto?
Toto was a mischievous rhesus monkey bought by Grandfather. He was playful, clever, and often created trouble in the house, which made him both interesting and difficult to handle.
Q2. Where did Grandfather buy Toto from?
Grandfather bought Toto from a tonga driver in Saharanpur. He paid a small amount for the monkey and decided to bring him home as a pet despite knowing he was very naughty.
Q3. How did Toto behave in the house?
Toto behaved very naughtily in the house. He broke dishes, tore clothes, and created chaos everywhere. His behaviour made it difficult for the family to manage him peacefully.
Q4. Where was Toto kept initially?
Initially, Toto was kept in a little closet or cage by Grandfather. This was done to control his mischievous activities and prevent him from damaging household items.
Q5. Why was Toto considered a troublesome pet?
Toto was considered troublesome because he was highly mischievous. He broke household items, disturbed other pets, and could not stay calm, making it difficult for the family to manage him properly.
Q6. What did Toto do during bath time?
During bath time, Toto created chaos in the bathroom. He jumped around, broke things, and made the place messy instead of behaving calmly like a normal pet.
Q7. How did Grandmother feel about Toto?
Grandmother was unhappy with Toto’s behaviour. She disliked the damage he caused in the house and often complained about his constant mischief and destruction of household items.
Q8. Where was Toto taken later?
Later, Toto was taken to Dehradun by Grandfather. He was carried carefully, but his mischievous behaviour continued during the journey, making things difficult for everyone involved.
Q9. What is the main nature of Toto?
Toto’s main nature was playful and mischievous. He loved creating trouble, breaking things, and disturbing everyone around him, which made him a very lively but difficult pet.
Q10. Why did Grandfather keep Toto as a pet?
Grandfather kept Toto as a pet because he was fond of animals and found Toto interesting and amusing. Despite his mischief, he enjoyed Toto’s playful behaviour and unusual personality.
Q11. Describe Toto’s behaviour in detail.
Toto was extremely mischievous and energetic. He would tear clothes, break utensils, and disturb everyone in the house. His actions often caused chaos, but he also entertained the family with his clever tricks and playful attitude, making him unforgettable.
Q12. How was Toto transported to Dehradun?
Toto was transported to Dehradun in a large basket or cage. Grandfather tried to keep him safe during the journey, but Toto’s restless nature made the trip difficult as he kept moving, jumping, and causing disturbance throughout.
Q13. Why was Toto hidden from others initially?
Toto was initially hidden because Grandfather did not want others to know about him. He feared that the family might not approve of keeping a monkey at home, especially due to his mischievous and destructive behaviour.
Q14. What problems did Toto create in the house?
Toto created many problems in the house by breaking dishes, tearing clothes, and disturbing other pets. His constant mischief made the house chaotic and difficult to manage for the family members.
Q15. How did Toto react to new surroundings?
Toto reacted to new surroundings with curiosity and excitement. He explored everything by touching, jumping, and sometimes breaking things, showing his restless and adventurous nature in every new place he visited.
Q16. Why was Toto finally removed from the house?
Toto was finally removed from the house because his mischief became uncontrollable. He caused too much damage to household items and disturbed everyone, making it impossible for the family to keep him safely.
Q17. What does Toto’s story teach us?
Toto’s story teaches us that wild animals are not suitable as pets. They require natural habitats and freedom, and keeping them at home can lead to problems for both humans and the animals themselves.
Q18. How did Toto behave during meals?
During meals, Toto behaved very badly. He would snatch food, eat quickly, and sometimes create mess around the dining area, making it difficult for others to enjoy their meals peacefully.
Q19. What kind of animal was Toto according to the story?
According to the story, Toto was a rhesus monkey known for its intelligence and mischievous behaviour. He was clever, energetic, and always ready to create trouble wherever he went.
Q20. Why is Toto remembered in the story?
Toto is remembered because of his unique personality and constant mischief. His playful actions and adventurous behaviour made him an unforgettable character in the story, leaving a lasting impression on the family.
Q1. Character Sketch of Toto
Introduction: Toto was a rhesus monkey bought by Grandfather in the story “The Adventure of Toto”. He is one of the most memorable characters due to his playful and mischievous nature.

Physical Appearance: Toto had bright, sparkling eyes and a lively face. His movements were quick and energetic, showing his alert and restless personality.

Nature and Behaviour: Toto was extremely mischievous and intelligent. He constantly created trouble by breaking utensils, tearing clothes, and disturbing everyone in the house. He could not sit quietly in one place and always looked for something to play with or destroy.

Habits: He had strange habits like throwing objects, biting things, and escaping from confinement. He enjoyed chaos and reacted strongly to new environments.

Conclusion: Toto was a lively but uncontrollable pet whose behaviour made him both entertaining and troublesome.
Q2. Grandfather’s Character Sketch
Introduction: Grandfather in “The Adventure of Toto” is portrayed as an animal lover who enjoys keeping pets, even unusual ones like Toto.

Animal Lover Nature: He had a deep affection for animals and could not resist bringing home Toto despite knowing his mischievous nature.

Decision Making: Grandfather was impulsive in his decisions. He bought Toto from a tonga driver without thinking about the consequences.

Patience Level: Initially, he tried to manage Toto’s behaviour, but as the monkey became more destructive, his patience decreased.

Conclusion: Grandfather is shown as kind-hearted but slightly careless, whose love for animals sometimes leads to problems.
Q3. Toto’s Mischievous Nature in Detail
Introduction: Toto’s mischievous behaviour is the central theme of the story “The Adventure of Toto”.

Destructive Activities: Toto frequently broke dishes, tore clothes, and damaged household items. His actions created chaos in the house.

Restless Behaviour: He could not stay calm in one place and always jumped from one object to another, disturbing everything around him.

Reaction to Environment: In new surroundings, Toto became even more active and destructive, showing curiosity mixed with mischief.

Conclusion: His behaviour made him entertaining but impossible to manage as a domestic pet.
Q4. Problems Created by Toto in the Household
Introduction: Toto caused many problems in the household due to his uncontrollable behaviour.

Damage to Property: He broke plates, glasses, and other household items, causing financial loss and inconvenience.

Disturbance to Family: His constant movement and mischief disturbed all family members, making it difficult to maintain peace at home.

Uncontrollable Nature: Toto could not be trained or controlled, which increased frustration among family members.

Conclusion: His presence made the house lively but chaotic, proving that wild animals are not suitable pets.
Q5. Moral of the Story “The Adventure of Toto”
Introduction: The story “The Adventure of Toto” conveys an important message about keeping wild animals as pets.

Lesson on Animal Nature: Wild animals like monkeys cannot be fully domesticated, no matter how much care is given.

Human Responsibility: Humans must understand their responsibility before bringing animals into their homes.

Impact of Wrong Decision: Grandfather’s decision to keep Toto leads to chaos, showing the consequences of impulsive choices.

Conclusion: The story teaches that animals belong in their natural habitat and should not be forced into domestic life.

About the Writer

Ruskin Bond

Ruskin Bond (born 1934) is a famous Indian author known for his simple, heart-touching, and nature-based stories. He is one of the most loved writers in Indian English literature, especially for children and young readers. Born in Kasauli, Himachal Pradesh, he spent most of his life in the hill stations of India, which strongly influence his writing style.

Bond is celebrated for his ability to bring everyday life, nature, animals, and human emotions alive through his stories. His writing style is simple, warm, and deeply engaging, making even ordinary events feel special and meaningful.

Some of his well-known works include The Blue Umbrella, The Room on the Roof, Rusty series, and many short stories for children. In the chapter "The Adventure of Toto", he humorously presents the mischievous nature of a monkey and highlights the fun as well as problems of keeping wild animals as pets.
