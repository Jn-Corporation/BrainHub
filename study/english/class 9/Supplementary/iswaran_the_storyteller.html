Conceptual Q&A Matrix

Q1. Who was Iswaran?
Iswaran was a loyal and imaginative assistant of Mahendra. He worked as a cook and helper at worksite camps. He was famous for his storytelling skills and dramatic narration style that made every story interesting and engaging.
Q2. Who was Mahendra?
Mahendra was a junior supervisor who worked at various worksite camps. He was a simple and practical man who depended on Iswaran for cooking and daily needs while staying away from home.
Q3. What was Iswaran famous for?
Iswaran was famous for his storytelling ability. He could turn simple incidents into dramatic and thrilling stories filled with imagination, suspense, and emotional expressions that fascinated everyone who listened to him.
Q4. How did Iswaran support Mahendra?
Iswaran supported Mahendra by cooking food, managing daily chores, and staying with him at isolated worksite camps. He also kept Mahendra entertained with his imaginative and dramatic storytelling skills.
Q5. What type of stories did Iswaran tell?
Iswaran told ghost stories, adventure tales, and dramatic incidents. He used expressive language and gestures, making even ordinary events sound mysterious, exciting, and full of suspense for his listeners.
Q6. How was Mahendra as a person?
Mahendra was a calm, simple, and hardworking person. He did not believe in superstitions and preferred to live a peaceful life, though he sometimes got influenced by Iswaran’s scary and imaginative stories.
Q7. Why did Mahendra hire Iswaran?
Mahendra hired Iswaran as a cook and helper because he needed someone to manage daily tasks at isolated worksite camps. Iswaran was reliable and also provided company through his storytelling.
Q8. What made Iswaran’s storytelling special?
Iswaran’s storytelling was special because of his dramatic style, emotional expressions, and ability to add suspense. He used imagination to make simple events sound thrilling, which kept his listener fully engaged.
Q9. How did Mahendra react to stories initially?
Initially, Mahendra enjoyed listening to Iswaran’s stories. He found them entertaining and interesting, though he did not always believe them completely, as he was a practical and logical thinker.
Q10. What was the central idea of Iswaran’s stories?
The central idea of Iswaran’s stories was imagination and drama. He transformed simple incidents into exciting tales filled with suspense, showing how storytelling can influence emotions and perception.
Q11. Describe Iswaran’s storytelling style in detail.
Iswaran’s storytelling style was highly dramatic and expressive. He used gestures, voice modulation, and vivid descriptions to bring stories to life. Even ordinary events became thrilling adventures filled with suspense and imagination, making listeners completely engaged and sometimes even scared.
Q12. How did Iswaran influence Mahendra?
Iswaran influenced Mahendra through his powerful storytelling. Although Mahendra was logical, repeated exposure to ghost stories and dramatic narration slowly affected his thoughts, making him feel uneasy at times, especially when he was alone in isolated camps.
Q13. What role did imagination play in the story?
Imagination played a very important role in the story. Iswaran used it to create thrilling and emotional narratives. His imaginative storytelling made simple incidents seem supernatural and exciting, showing how powerful imagination can influence human perception.
Q14. Why did Mahendra stay in isolated camps?
Mahendra stayed in isolated worksite camps because of his job as a junior supervisor. These camps were away from towns and cities, which made them quiet and lonely, increasing his dependence on Iswaran for company and entertainment.
Q15. How did Iswaran describe the tiger incident?
Iswaran described the tiger incident in a highly dramatic way, making it sound terrifying and real. He used suspenseful narration and exaggerated details, turning a simple encounter into a thrilling story that impressed and frightened listeners.
Q16. What effect did stories have on Mahendra?
Iswaran’s stories had a psychological effect on Mahendra. Although he tried to stay rational, the vivid descriptions of ghosts and supernatural events sometimes made him uneasy, especially during nights in the lonely camps.
Q17. How is Iswaran different from Mahendra?
Iswaran is imaginative, expressive, and dramatic, while Mahendra is practical, logical, and calm. Their contrasting personalities create an interesting dynamic, where storytelling meets rational thinking in the isolated environment they live in.
Q18. What does the story teach about storytelling?
The story teaches that storytelling has the power to influence emotions and imagination. A good storyteller can make simple events seem extraordinary, but excessive imagination can sometimes blur the line between reality and fiction.
Q19. Why is Iswaran an important character?
Iswaran is important because he represents imagination and creativity. His storytelling ability adds depth and excitement to the story, showing how one person’s words can change the mood and thinking of others.
Q20. What is the central message of the story?
The central message of the story is that imagination is powerful and can strongly influence human emotions. It also shows the balance between reality and fiction, and how storytelling can shape perception and thinking.

About the Writer

R.K. Laxman

R.K. Laxman (1921–2015) was one of India’s most famous cartoonists, illustrators, and writers. He is best known for creating the iconic character “The Common Man,” which appeared in his daily newspaper cartoon series “You Said It” in The Times of India. His work beautifully captured the everyday struggles, humour, and irony of Indian society.

Apart from cartoons, Laxman also wrote short stories that reflected humour, imagination, and human behaviour. His writing style is simple, witty, and deeply observant of people’s personalities and actions. He had a unique ability to present serious social issues in a light and humorous way.

The story “Iswaran the Storyteller” reflects his talent for humour and storytelling, showing how imagination and exaggeration can turn ordinary life into something entertaining and memorable.
